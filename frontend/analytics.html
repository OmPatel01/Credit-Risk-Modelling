Risk Analytics & ECL

Analyze portfolio risk composition and calculate Expected Credit Loss

📚 Key Concepts

Risk Segmentation:
Groups borrowers into risk buckets (Low, Medium, High) based on PD. Simplifies portfolio analysis and decision-making by providing a high-level overview.
Expected Credit Loss (ECL):
Estimated financial loss from loan defaults. Calculated as: ECL = PD × LGD × EAD
  • PD (Probability of Default): Likelihood of borrower default
  • LGD (Loss Given Default): Percentage of exposure lost if default occurs (typically 45%)
  • EAD (Exposure at Default): Outstanding loan amount at time of default
Required for IFRS 9 regulatory compliance.

📌 Model Assumptions
  • Probability of Default (PD) is user-provided or edited in the table
  • Loss Given Default (LGD) is user-defined (default: 45%)
  • Exposure at Default (EAD) is taken from loan amount
  • Expected Credit Loss is computed as ECL = PD × LGD × EAD
  • Risk segmentation is based on PD thresholds (Low, Medium, High)
  • Segment-level aggregation groups borrowers by risk category
